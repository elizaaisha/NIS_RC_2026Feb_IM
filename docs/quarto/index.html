Cardiovascular Complications In Patients Undergoing Lung Transplantation: A National Inpatient Sample Study

Author
Affiliation

Eliza Aisha

Dow University of Health Sciences

Preamble:

  • Study Objective: To evaluate the burden and outcomes of cardiovascular complications occurring during lung transplant hospitalization and identify patient- and hospital-level predictors associated with adverse clinical outcomes.

  • Data Source: Cross-sectional analysis of the National Inpatient Sample (NIS) from 2018 to 2021. The NIS is part of the Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project (HCUP) and provides a stratified sample of inpatient discharges from U.S. community hospitals across all payers.

  • Cohort Definition: Patients were included if they met the following criteria

    • Age ≥ 18 years

    • Undergoing lung transplant (ICD 10 PCS: 0BYC0Z0, 0BYC0Z1, 0BYC0Z2, 0BYD0Z0, 0BYD0Z1, 0BYD0Z2, 0BYF0Z0, 0BYF0Z1, 0BYF0Z2, 0BYG0Z0, 0BYG0Z1, 0BYG0Z2, 0BYH0Z0, 0BYH0Z1, 0BYH0Z2, 0BYJ0Z0, 0BYJ0Z1, 0BYJ0Z2, 0BYK0Z0, 0BYK0Z1, 0BYK0Z2, 0BYL0Z0, 0BYL0Z1, 0BYL0Z2, 0BYM0Z0, 0BYM0Z1, 0BYM0Z2)

  • Primary Outcomes:

    • In-hospital all-cause mortality

    • MACE (Acute myocardial infarction OR Cardiac arrest OR Cardiac arrest OR Ventricular arrhythmia OR Revascularization OR Mechanical circulatory support)

    • Total hospital length of stay (days)

    • Total charge, inflation adjusted to 2021 ($)

  • Secondary Outcomes:

    • Acute myocardial infarction

    • Ischemic stroke

    • Ventricular arrhythmia

    • Cardiogenic shock

    • Venous thromboembolism

    • Mechanical circulatory support

    • Sepsis

    • Acute kidney injury

    • Non-home discharge

  • Covariates for Adjustment: All models adjusted for the following:

    • Demographics: Age, sex, race, income, insurance, location of patient’s residence, hospital region, and hospital bedsize

    • Medical History: Chronic heart failure, diabetes mellitus, hypertension, chronic kidney disease, peripheral vascular disease, liver disease, valvular heart disease, anemia, and pulmonary circulatory disorders, grouped charlson comorbidity index.

  • Statistical Methods:

    • Univariable Analysis:

      • Categorical variables: Compared using Pearson’s chi-square test with Rao & Scott adjustment

      • Continuous variables: Analyzed using the design-based Kruskal–Wallis test, a non-parametric alternative suitable for survey-weighted data

    • Multivariable Analysis:

      • Binary outcomes (e.g., mortality): Survey-weighted logistic regression using the quasibinomial family

      • Continuous outcomes (e.g., LOS, hospital charges): Survey-weighted linear regression using the gaussian family

      • Results reported as adjusted odds ratios (aORs) or beta coefficients with 95% confidence intervals

  • Software: All statistical analyses were performed using the R Statistical Language (Version 4.5.0; R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria), incorporating survey-weighted procedures via the survey package to account for the complex sampling design of NIS.
